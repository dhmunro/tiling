Guide for rhomb tiling explorer

Interactive controls

  • Drag with mouse or touch scrolls vertically or horizontally.
  • Mouse wheel or pinch gesture zooms in or out.
  • Double click pops up control panel. Click the X at its upper right corner to close the controls.

Control Panel

  • Magnify buttons zoom in (+) or out (-) about the center of the screen. (Wheel or gesture zoom is centered at the touch position.)
  • Fullscreen button enters or exits fullscreen mode. This is a great way to study the patterns without any distractions.
  • nsym spinner allows you to set the number of grid line directions in the multigrid. For even numbers, there are nsym/2 different rhomb shapes, always including a square; for odd nsym, (nsym-1)/2 rhomb shapes.
  • spokes checkbox lets you look at a radially symmetric multigrid as suggested by Lutfalla and implemented on the pattern collider page. With spokes checkbox off, you get patterns with grid phases chosen in a manner analogous to Boyle. For the nsym = 4, 5, and 6 cases, the no-spokes are the well-known self-similar tilings, while the spokes patterns are not.
  • grid checkbox toggle visibility of the multigrid itself.
  • predefined menu lets you select one of several 5-color palettes.
  • reverse checkbox reverses the order of palette colors
  • swap checkbox swaps colors 0 and 1, and colors 2 and 4.
  • index checkbox uses two colors for each of the two rhomb shapes in the nsym=4 and 5 tilings, and for two of the three rhomb shapes in the nsym=6 and 7 tilings, and does nothing for larger nsym. The extra colors generally are prettier, but may make it more difficult to understand the pattern of shapes.
  • custom text box allows you to specify custom grid color, edge color or tile palette colors. The format is grid+edge-color0-color1-color2-color3-color4... The leading "grid+" is optional, but if there is no "+" or "-", then the single color is taken to be the grid color. You may specify only "grid+edge" to only customize the grid and edge colors. You must specify at least five palette colors if you specify any. More than five colors are useful for nsym greater than 11, otherwise they will not be seen.

URL query parameters

You can add URL query parameters to the page address to initialize the tiling with some pattern besides the default nsym=5 blue palette. For example, dhmunro.github.io/tiling?nsym=9&theme=pastels sets nsym to 9 and selects the pastells color palette. Recognized parameters are:

  • nsym=number
  • edge=number of pixels initial rhomb edge length sets initial zoom level
  • init=perfect to set the spokes checkbox
  • grid=csscolor to set the grid checkbox, and also to set the color of the grid lines
  • theme=palettename chooses initial color palette. An optional R suffix reverses the palette, an optional S suffix sets the index checkbox, and an optional B suffix sets the swap checkbox. (If there are multiple suffixes, they must appear in RSB order.)
  • theme=edge-color0-color1-color2-color3-color4... defines and sets a custom palette. The colors can be in hex rgb or rrggbb form, or CSS color names like peachpuff or skyblue. The first is the edge color. You must specify at least five palette colors after the edge. More colors will only begin to show up for nsym greater than 11.
